# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone in the CitizenWeather community will be treated with respect and dignity, regardless of their background, experience level, identity, or affiliations.

We are dedicated to creating a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:
- Age, body size, caste, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression
- Level of experience, education, socio-economic status
- Nationality, personal appearance, political beliefs, or religion
- Sexual identity and orientation, or any other dimension of identity

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Helping and supporting newer members
- Assuming good intent while addressing issues respectfully

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- Deliberate misgendering or exclusion
- Unwelcome sexual attention or physical contact
- Insulting or derogatory comments, including personal attacks
- Threats of violence or violent language
- Posting others' private information without consent
- Spamming, trolling, or deliberately disrupting discussions
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All spaces managed by the CitizenWeather community and its projects
- Public and private repositories
- Issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Comments and code reviews
- Community events and gatherings
- Official social media accounts
- Any space representing the community

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to **conduct@citizenweather.org**. 

When reporting, please include:
- Description of the incident
- When and where it occurred
- Any relevant context or witnesses
- How you were affected

All reports will be handled confidentially. We do not tolerate retaliation against reporters.

### Consequences

Community leaders will investigate reports and determine appropriate responses. Possible actions include:

- **Warning** — A private conversation addressing the behavior
- **Temporary Ban** — Removal from community spaces for a set period
- **Permanent Ban** — Removal from the community and projects
- **Escalation** — Involvement of law enforcement if necessary

The goal is to help people understand the impact of their behavior and foster positive change.

## Appeals

Those who believe a decision was made in error may submit a written appeal within 30 days.
